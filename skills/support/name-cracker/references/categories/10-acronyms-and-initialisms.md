# Acronyms and initialisms

Names built from the initial letters of a longer phrase. When the letters combine into something pronounceable as a single word, it's an acronym; when each letter is read out individually, it's an initialism. Both compress a technical or institutional phrase into something short enough to say or type constantly.

## Techniques

- Form a pronounceable word out of initial letters, an acronym (NASA, LASER, RADAR, YAML)
- Spell out each initial letter individually, an initialism, when the letters don't form a sayable word (IBM, API, AWS, CSS)
- Favor letter sequences that happen to spell an existing or word-like string for extra memorability (SCUBA, BASIC)
- Reserve the compressed form for domains where the audience will learn the expansion through repeated technical use (SDK, JVM, REST)

## Examples

NASA, BASIC, LASER, YAML, IBM, AWS, CUDA

## Pitfalls

- Both forms are opaque without their expansion, so first exposure conveys no meaning at all.
- Memorability stays low until the expanded phrase becomes widely known.
- The chosen letters may collide with a different well-known acronym in an adjacent field.
