# People and cultural references

The name is a real or fictional person's name, or a reference to a specific character, book, film, or cultural artifact, giving the product a personality and a ready-made story that a coined word can't supply.

## Techniques

- Use a founder's surname directly (Dell, McAfee, Jenkins)
- Use an ordinary first name to humanize an assistant or product (Alexa, Siri, Ada)
- Borrow a historical figure's name for its associated authority or trait (Ada, after Ada Lovelace)
- Borrow a literary or fictional character for its tone or story (Kafka, Cassandra, Gatsby, Sherlock, Jekyll)
- Borrow another project's naming lineage as a reference chain (Hadoop, named after a toy elephant; Moby, Docker's whale)

## Examples

Mercedes, Dell, Jenkins, Alexa, Siri, Ada, Gatsby, Kafka, Hadoop, Moby, Sherlock, Jekyll

## Pitfalls

- Personal names raise ownership, trademark, and cultural-sensitivity questions.
- A cultural reference can be misread, or carry an unintended connotation, if the audience doesn't share it.
