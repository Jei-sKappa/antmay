# Compound words

Two or more complete, recognizable words joined into a single identity. Because every part is already a real word, the result combines the familiarity of plain language with the distinctiveness of a name no one has used before.

## Techniques

- Closed compound: fuse two words with no space or joint (Cloudflare)
- Open compound: keep two words separate but bind them as one identity (Stack Overflow)
- Hyphenated compound: connect two words with a hyphen (Coca-Cola)
- CamelCase compound: capitalize the second word inside a single unbroken token (YouTube)
- Pair a generic category word with a distinctive qualifier so the whole reads as one unit (DigitalOcean, WordPress)

## Examples

GitHub, Facebook, Cloudflare, WordPress, Stack Overflow, DigitalOcean

## Pitfalls

- Joining two full words can produce a name that is long or awkward to say.
- The pattern is easy to imitate, so competitors often land on nearly identical [word]+[word] compounds.
