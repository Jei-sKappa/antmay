# Symbolic names

The name is an abstract word standing for a value, aspiration, or feeling the brand wants to be associated with, rather than for any concrete feature of the product.

## Techniques

- Name after a virtue the brand wants to project: trust, precision, freedom (Fidelity, Equinix)
- Name after an abstract capability the product embodies: intelligence, connection, scale (OpenAI, Anthropic)
- Name after a mythic or literary object standing for hidden power or knowledge (Palantir)
- Name after a felt quality rather than a mechanism: harmony, unity, calm (Unity, Harmony)

## Examples

Unity, Harmony, Palantir, OpenAI, Anthropic, Fidelity, Equinix

## Pitfalls

- Abstract-virtue names are heavily crowded across unrelated industries.
- The intended value doesn't connect to the product on its own — it needs supporting marketing to land.
