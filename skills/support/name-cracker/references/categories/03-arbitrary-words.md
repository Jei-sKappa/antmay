# Arbitrary dictionary words

Existing, ordinary words picked up and used in a context that has nothing to do with their normal meaning. The word is real and familiar, so it reads as instantly memorable and highly brandable, even though it tells the audience nothing about the product on first contact.

## Techniques

- Pick a short, everyday noun from outside your industry and let the product redefine it (Apple, Amazon)
- Borrow a word from an adjacent domain of learning or craft — mythology, gemstones, language names (Oracle, Ruby, Python)
- Choose a word purely for its phonetic quality and let its dictionary meaning stay dormant (Vercel)
- Favor short, common words over long or technical ones, since the whole appeal is effortless recall

## Examples

Apple, Amazon, Oracle, Python, Ruby, Vercel

## Pitfalls

- Domains for common words are almost always taken, and search results get diluted by the word's original meaning.
- The word's legacy connotation can clash with or distract from the product's actual positioning.
