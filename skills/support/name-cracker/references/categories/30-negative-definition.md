# Negative-definition names

Names that state what the product is not, defining it by exclusion or opposition rather than by direct description. The technique often pairs with a recursive or self-referential twist, where the name argues with its own category label.

## Techniques

- Recursive negation naming the product against its own acronym (GNU, expanding to "GNU's Not Unix")
- Explicit "Is Not a/an X" phrasing (WINE, "Wine Is Not an Emulator"; YAML, "YAML Ain't Markup Language")
- `No + category` prefix rejecting an established category outright (NoSQL)
- `Un + verb/noun` prefix undoing an expectation (Unbounce, Unsplash)

## Examples

GNU, WINE, YAML, NoSQL, Unbounce, Unsplash

## Pitfalls

- The name defines the product only relative to something else, carrying no meaning of its own once the reference point is forgotten.
- Recursive or negated acronyms can confuse anyone unfamiliar with the underlying joke.
