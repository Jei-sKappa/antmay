# Letter-plus-word names

A single letter is fused onto an ordinary word as a modifier, abbreviation, or phonetic stand-in, letting one character carry a whole category signal such as electronic, cross-platform, or personal. The letter acts as a compact prefix rather than a full word of its own.

## Techniques

- Prefix `i-` for personal or consumer-facing devices (iPhone)
- Prefix `e-` for "electronic" or "online" (eBay)
- Prefix `x-` for cross-platform or "extra" (Xcode)
- Prefix `g-` or `my-` as a brand or personalization marker (G Suite)
- Leading initial standing in for a tool's category or maintainer (CMake, F-Droid, RStudio)

## Examples

iPhone, eBay, Xcode, G Suite, CMake, F-Droid, RStudio

## Pitfalls

- Prefix trends date quickly — an `i-` or `e-` name instantly signals the era it was coined in.
- Once the trend fades, the letter can read as meaningless decoration rather than added meaning.
