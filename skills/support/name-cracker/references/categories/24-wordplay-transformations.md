# Wordplay transformations

Names produced by mechanically transforming a source word's letters — reversing it, rearranging it, or choosing one that reads the same in both directions. The result hides or preserves a reference to the source while producing a visually or phonetically novel string.

## Techniques

- Reverse a meaningful word to hide it in plain sight (Harpo from Oprah reversed, Nevaeh from "heaven" reversed)
- Reverse a keyword for an internal codename with a hidden meaning
- Rearrange the letters of a founder's or company's name into a new pronounceable word (anagram)
- Choose or construct a word that reads identically forwards and backwards (Civic, Level, Radar, Kayak, Ava)

## Examples

Harpo, Nevaeh, Civic, Level, Radar, Kayak, Ava

## Pitfalls

- Reversal often produces awkward or unpronounceable results once the word is flipped.
- Good, pronounceable anagrams are rare — most letter rearrangements are unusable.
- The palindrome vocabulary is inherently narrow, so options run out fast.
