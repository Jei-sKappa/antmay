# Suggestive names

Names that imply a benefit, behavior, or quality without describing the product literally. The name works through association: it points at a feeling or outcome rather than naming the mechanism, which keeps the brand meaningful but flexible as the product evolves.

## Techniques

- Borrow a metaphor for the desired quality rather than the function itself (Slack → looseness and flexibility, Lighthouse → guidance and diagnostics)
- Name the feeling the product should produce rather than what it does (Zoom → fast motion, Notion → a captured idea)
- Pick a word from an adjacent domain that gestures at the product's role without stating it (Stripe → the strip running through payment infrastructure)
- Reuse a familiar word so the suggestion rides on its existing connotation (GitHub → a hub for collaborative work)

## Examples

Slack, Stripe, Zoom, Notion, GitHub, Lighthouse

## Pitfalls

- The intended association may not be obvious until the market has learned it through repeated exposure.
- Suggestive names can feel interchangeable with names from unrelated categories that chase the same vague quality.
