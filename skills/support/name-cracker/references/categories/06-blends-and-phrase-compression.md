# Blends and phrase compression

Names built by fusing or shortening the pieces of a longer word or phrase rather than concatenating whole words. Unlike a plain compound, at least one source word is altered, truncated, or reduced to a syllable, which produces something more compact and often more pronounceable than the original.

## Techniques

- Blend two words together, truncating or altering at least one so they fuse into a single new word (Pinterest = pin + interest, Groupon = group + coupon, Netflix = internet + flicks)
- Combine the initial syllables of each word in a phrase, rather than initial letters (Microsoft = microcomputer + software, Instagram = instant + telegram)
- Compress an official multi-word name into a short, pronounceable phrase (FedEx = Federal Express, Cisco = San Francisco, Intel = Integrated Electronics, SoFi = Social Finance)
- Clip a longer word down to its front, back, or middle fragment (app from application, phone from telephone)

## Examples

Microsoft, Pinterest, Instagram, Groupon, Netflix, FedEx, Cisco, Intel, SoFi

## Pitfalls

- The fusion can feel forced if it obscures both source words beyond recognition.
- Short, compressed forms may already be heavily used or claimed elsewhere.
- Once the blended form becomes common, its original meaning tends to disappear from everyday use.
