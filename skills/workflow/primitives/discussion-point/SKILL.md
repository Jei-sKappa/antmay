# Discussion Point

Frame one concrete decision into a self-contained discussion point: a compact structure that gives the reader everything needed to settle a single choice, and nothing that belongs to a broader conversation. You receive one point from the caller and either present it in chat or write it into a file the caller names. You never invent the decision, batch several points together, or expand into a wider discussion.

## Precondition and refusal

Act only when the caller supplies one concrete point to work on. A concrete point is a single decision that is genuinely open — the caller identifies the choice at stake and the surrounding evidence.

Refuse when:

- no concrete point is supplied, or the request is a vague topic rather than a specific decision — ask the caller for the one decision to frame, and do not fabricate it;
- more than one independent decision is bundled into a single request — ask the caller to hand them over one at a time;
- you are asked to emit into a file but no target path is given — ask the caller for the path, and do not choose one yourself.

When you refuse, name exactly what is missing and stop. Do not substitute your own judgment for the caller's decision, and do not turn the request into an open-ended interview.

## Point structure

A discussion point has these parts, in this order:

- **Title** — a short line naming the decision.
- **Point** — the decision to make, stated as a single question or fork. This is the choice the reader must settle, not the background.
- **What you need to know** — the self-contained evidence and surrounding context a reader needs to decide, written so the point stands on its own without any prior conversation. State established facts plainly as facts.
- **Creative options OR a practical proposed solution** — choose the presentation that fits the fork:
  - *Creative options* when the decision is genuinely open and the reader benefits from seeing distinct, well-formed alternatives with their trade-offs. Present each option as a real candidate, not a strawman.
  - *A practical proposed solution* when one path is clearly the sensible default and the reader mainly needs to confirm or redirect it. State the single proposal and what it entails.
- **Recommendation** — your reasoned pick with its principal trade-off. Give one clear recommendation; do not hedge across several. If no option is genuinely preferable, say so and explain why the choice is a true toss-up.

## Discipline

- **Separate established facts from open decisions.** Anything already settled, observed, or constrained is stated as a fact inside *What you need to know* — never dressed up as an option. Only genuine choices, where reasonable people could decide differently, are presented as options or a proposal. Do not manufacture alternatives around a fact to make the point look like a decision.
- **One point at a time.** Present or emit a single decision per invocation. If the caller's material actually contains several independent decisions, that is a refusal condition (see above), not a reason to produce a batch.
- **The point is transient framing, not a durable record.** It carries the options menu, recommendation, and deliberation needed to reach a choice — material that has no lasting value once the choice is made. Producing the settled decision's durable record is the caller's job, not yours.

## Operating modes

The caller drives which mode applies.

### Interactive presentation

Present the point in chat using the structure above, then stop and let the human decide. Ask no unrelated questions and open no side threads. The presentation is complete when the reader has the Title, Point, What you need to know, the options or proposed solution, and your Recommendation for that one decision.

### Normalization and emission

Take a caller-supplied point and write it, in the canonical structure above, into the file path the caller provides. The caller owns path allocation, the correctness of the evidence handed to you, and any surrounding document structure the file lives in; you own only that the emitted point is well-formed and self-contained. Normalize the caller's raw material into the structure — tighten wording, order the parts, and make the point stand alone — without changing which decision is being made or introducing evidence the caller did not supply. Do not create or choose the target path.
