# Distorted spellings

Take an existing, recognizable word and alter its spelling — dropping letters, swapping them, or misspelling it outright — while keeping it close enough to the original that it stays pronounceable and familiar. The distortion mainly buys domain availability and visual distinctiveness without losing the anchor word's recognition.

## Techniques

- Remove vowels entirely or drop a trailing vowel (Flickr, Tumblr, Scribd, Grindr)
- Double a letter for texture or emphasis (Dribbble)
- Substitute one letter for another for visual or phonetic effect: `C→K`, `I→Y`, `S→Z`, `Ex→X` (Lyft, Xero)
- Respell a word the way it actually sounds when spoken (Reddit from "read it", Digg)
- Deliberately misspell a familiar word while preserving its sound (Google from "googol", Topix)

## Examples

Flickr, Tumblr, Lyft, Reddit, Digg, Scribd, Google, Grindr, Xero

## Pitfalls

- The spelling often needs to be explained or spelled out loud, repeatedly, to new users.
- Users may default back to typing the standard spelling out of habit, missing the actual name.
- Heavy vowel removal can lower readability and leave pronunciation ambiguous.
