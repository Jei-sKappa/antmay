# Mythological and legendary names

The name is drawn from mythology, religion, or folklore, importing an established story and a ready-made set of associations that the product doesn't have to build from scratch.

## Techniques

- Name after a titan or god tied to a relevant power: strength, light, fire (Atlas, Apollo, Prometheus)
- Name after a trickster or storm figure for something fast or disruptive (Loki, Thor)
- Name after a messenger or threshold figure for connective or transitional tools (Hermes, Janus)
- Name after a figure of prophecy or rebirth for predictive or resilient systems (Cassandra, Phoenix)

## Examples

Atlas, Apollo, Prometheus, Cassandra, Loki, Thor, Hermes, Janus, Phoenix

## Pitfalls

- The most recognizable mythological names are already claimed across infrastructure and open source.
- The figure's own story can carry a connotation that clashes with the product (Cassandra's prophecies were never believed; Loki is a trickster).
