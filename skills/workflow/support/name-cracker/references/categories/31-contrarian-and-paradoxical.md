# Contrarian and paradoxical names

Names that deliberately clash with the language a category expects, or combine two apparently contradictory concepts into one phrase. Both moves trade the comfort of an obvious fit for the jolt of surprise, betting that the mismatch itself becomes the memorable hook.

## Techniques

- Pick a word with a negative or unexpected connotation for a serious category (Rust for a systems language, Chaos for a testing tool)
- Borrow a word from a looser, more casual register than the category expects (Slack, Discord)
- Combine two contradictory concepts into a single working phrase (Virtual Reality, Serverless)
- Pair an adjective and noun that cancel each other out (Tiny Giant, Silent Signal, Friendly Fire, Open Secret)

## Examples

Rust, Slack, Discord, Chaos, Boring Company, Virtual Reality, Serverless, Tiny Giant, Silent Signal, Open Secret

## Pitfalls

- The negative or contradictory association can dominate first impressions instead of landing as clever.
- Paradoxical phrasing can confuse readers who take the words at face value.
