# Geographic names

The name is taken from a city, region, landmark, river, mountain, or other real or fictional place, grounding the product in a location that carries its own scale or character.

## Techniques

- Use an exact place name directly (Amazon, Adobe)
- Use an abbreviated or altered place name (Cisco, from San Francisco)
- Use a place name whose meaning is indirect or folk-etymological rather than literal (MongoDB, associated with "humongous" rather than geography)
- Use a landmark or natural feature for scale or grandeur (Everest, Tahoe, Alpine)
- Use a historical or fictional place name for character (Red Hat's Fedora ecosystem)

## Examples

Amazon, Cisco, Adobe, Alpine, MongoDB, Tahoe, Everest, Fedora

## Pitfalls

- A specific place can unintentionally constrain how broad or global the product feels.
- Local or cultural resonance in the place of origin may not travel to other markets.
