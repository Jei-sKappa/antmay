# Onomatopoeic names

Names that imitate a sound or physical sensation the product produces, so the word itself sounds like the action it names — zooming, clicking, snapping. The effect is vivid and immediate, tapping recognition that bypasses literal definition.

## Techniques

- Short, punchy sound-word standing for speed or lightness (Zip, Zoom, Snap)
- Sound-word for a physical interaction or click event (Click, Ping, Pop)
- Repeated or rhythmic sound imitation (TikTok)

## Examples

Zoom, Zip, Ping, Click, TikTok, Pop, Snap

## Pitfalls

- Sound-imitating words tend to be short and common, so they're frequently already taken.
- The category is crowded — many products reach for the same handful of vivid sounds.
