# Foreign and classical roots

The name borrows a real foreign word, or assembles Latin- and Greek-sounding roots from scratch, giving the product an international or timeless feel without needing to be a real word in any language.

## Techniques

- Borrow a real foreign word directly for its meaning or sound (Ubuntu, Toyota, Asana, Akamai)
- Construct a hybrid from Latin-derived elements without using an actual foreign word (Lenovo, Vercel)
- Build a pseudo-classical name from recognizable roots such as `neo-`, `nova-`, `lumen-`, `nexus-`, `omni-` (Oculus, Nexora, Lumina, Consul)
- Blend roots from more than one language family into a single distinctive, ownable word (Lumino)

## Examples

Ubuntu, Toyota, Asana, Akamai, Vercel, Oculus, Lumina, Nexora, Consul

## Pitfalls

- Pronunciation, meaning, or connotation can differ sharply across markets and languages.
- Pseudo-classical constructions can end up sounding generic or interchangeable with countless similarly built names.
