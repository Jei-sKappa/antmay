# Affixed names

A root word — often a plain verb or noun describing the product's domain — modified by attaching a prefix or suffix. The affix does the work of turning an ordinary word into something distinctive while keeping the root's meaning legible.

## Techniques

- Attach `-ify` to a verb-like root to suggest transformation (Shopify, Spotify)
- Attach `-ly` to a root for a light, approachable feel (Grammarly, Bitly)
- Attach a category-signaling suffix such as `-able`, `-base`, `-hub`, `-stack`, or `-labs` (Airtable, Firebase, GitHub, Fullstack, GitLab-style naming)
- Prepend a modifier prefix such as `neo-`, `hyper-`, `meta-`, `micro-`, `open-`, `super-`, or `re-` to signal an extended or modernized version of a known concept (Kubernetes-adjacent naming, Reactify)

## Examples

Shopify, Spotify, Grammarly, Bitly, Kubernetes, Reactify

## Pitfalls

- Fashionable suffixes cluster together and quickly date the name to a specific naming-trend era.
- Overused suffixes reduce distinctiveness once many competitors reach for the same pattern.
