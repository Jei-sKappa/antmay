# Sound patterns

Names built around a pleasing arrangement of sound — repeated syllables, rhyming pairs, or matching initial consonants — so the name sticks in memory through rhythm rather than through meaning. The words involved don't need to relate to the product at all; the appeal is purely acoustic.

## Techniques

- Reduplicate a syllable or whole word (TikTok, BlaBlaCar, Badoo)
- Double a letter or sound to add rhythmic texture (Dribbble, Yahoo, Google)
- Alliterate by pairing words that share an initial sound (PayPal, Coca-Cola, Best Buy, Firefox)
- Rhyme or near-rhyme two syllables or words against each other (StubHub, LeanBean, HandyAndy)

## Examples

TikTok, PayPal, Google, Yahoo, BlaBlaCar, StubHub, Dribbble

## Pitfalls

- The playful rhythm can read as informal in contexts that call for gravitas.
- A sound pattern alone carries no semantic content, so it needs heavy repetition before it sticks.
