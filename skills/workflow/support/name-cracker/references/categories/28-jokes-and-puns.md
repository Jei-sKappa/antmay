# Jokes and puns

Names that rely on wordplay, double meanings, or in-group developer humor rather than direct description. The name works as a joke first and a label second, rewarding people who get the reference with a small moment of delight.

## Techniques

- Double-meaning word that also happens to fit the tool's function (Git, playing on British slang alongside version control)
- Borrow a real-world craft or brewing term for a package tool (Homebrew, Yarn, Bun)
- Shorten a common word into a knowing wink (pip, sudo)
- Pick a homophone or near-homophone with comic intent (Lettuce)
- Build a whole themed joke family across an ecosystem (Mocha, Chai, Sinon, CoffeeScript)

## Examples

Git, Homebrew, Yarn, Bun, pip, sudo, Lettuce, Mocha, Chai, Sinon

## Pitfalls

- The joke can obscure what the project actually does, especially for newcomers outside the in-group.
- Humor that lands in one community may read as unserious or simply confusing in another.
